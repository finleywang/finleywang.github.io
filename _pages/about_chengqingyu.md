Chengqing Yu is currently a PhD student at the Institute of Computing Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS), where he is affiliated with the Research Center for Intelligent Equipment Systems and the State Key Laboratory of AI Safety, under the supervision of **Prof. Yongjun Xu**, with additional co-supervision by **Prof. Fei Wang**. His research interests include incomplete time series modeling, spatial-temporal data mining, and AI4Science. He has published over 30 papers in top-tier journals (e.g., The Innovation, TKDE, Information Fusion) and conferences (e.g., NeurIPS, KDD, AAAI, CIKM). His work has been cited more than 2000 times according to Google Scholar. Notably, two of his papers—GinAR and DSFormer—were recognized as the most influential papers by PaperDigest, and one of his paper has been featured as Highly Cited Papers in ESI. He has served as a Program Committee (PC) member and reviewer for prestigious conferences and journals, including NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, KDD, TKDE, TITS, IJCAI, MM, AAAI, and others. In addition to his academic contributions, he is a contributor to the popular time-series analysis project BasicTS, which has attracted over 1,500 stars on GitHub.
